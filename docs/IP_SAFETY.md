# IP Safety

WC26 Hub is a fan-made card and schedule tool. It must stay visually original and must not imply an official relationship with FIFA, the World Cup, teams, players, broadcasters, or tournament organizers.

## Allowed Assets

- Player names as text in editorial, factual, stat, prediction, or debate context
- Country names
- Flag emojis
- Shirt numbers
- Generic jersey-back silhouettes
- Original nation-inspired shirt designs
- Plain text
- Original layouts
- Original abstract football-inspired graphics
- Generic pitch lines, stadium glow, spotlights, arcs, grids, and geometric shapes
- User-provided assets only when the user confirms they have rights to use them
- Licensed assets only after license terms are reviewed and documented

## Not Allowed

- FIFA logos
- Official World Cup logos
- Official tournament artwork
- Official team crests or federation marks
- Sponsor logos
- Nike, Adidas, Puma, or other manufacturer logos
- Real kit copies
- Club or national-team shirt artwork
- Player photos
- Realistic player likenesses or AI-generated lookalikes
- Cartoon versions that clearly identify a real player's face
- Broadcast graphics or screenshots
- Visual identities copied from sports media brands, live-score apps, or tournament organizers
- Any copy that claims official partnership, endorsement, or affiliation

## Visual Identity Rules

- Use original dark stadium glow, gold energy lines, pitch-grid patterns, spotlight gradients, and abstract ball arcs.
- Do not mimic official World Cup branding, trophy silhouettes, mascots, posters, or broadcast packages.
- Keep typography bold and sports-native, but use available system fonts and original composition.
- Keep cards logo-free by default. If licensed logos or photos are added later, keep them behind an explicit asset-rights review.
- Back-of-jersey cards may use an original shirt silhouette, custom name, shirt number, and nation-inspired colors. Do not add crests, sponsor marks, manufacturer logos, official kit patterns, or player likenesses.

## Disclaimer Guidance

Use subtle copy where appropriate:

```text
Fan-made. Not affiliated with FIFA or any official tournament organizer.
```

The disclaimer should be visible enough to reduce confusion but small enough that it does not ruin shareability.

## Future Licensed Asset Plan

If the product later uses official logos, crests, player images, or branded tournament marks:

1. Confirm the license source and permitted usage.
2. Store license notes with the asset.
3. Add a clear asset attribution field if required.
4. Keep a logo-free fallback template.
5. Re-run a visual review to avoid implying broader official affiliation.
