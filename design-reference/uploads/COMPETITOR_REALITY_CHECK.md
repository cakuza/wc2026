# Competitor Reality Check

Date: 2026-06-01

The product does not beat live-score products at live scores. It must win as a country-first, local-time, share-card fan utility.

## Google Search

- What they do better: instant answers, massive trust, live snippets, no learning curve.
- What we do better: country hub, fan-card creation, captions, all-team share flows.
- Why users add us: they want to save/send a country's road, not just look up a score.
- Wedge: "Pick your country. Create fan cards."
- Still weak: Google wins factual lookup until our data is official and current.

## SofaScore

- What they do better: live scores, lineups, player stats, notifications, app habit.
- What we do better: social poster creation and fan-made local-time cards.
- Why users add us: they want content to share, not only match state.
- Wedge: schedule-to-card workflow.
- Still weak: live data, depth, and user habit.

## FotMob

- What they do better: live match UX, news, notifications, player ratings.
- What we do better: simple 48-team fan card studio and WhatsApp/X captions.
- Why users add us: fan pages and group chats need assets.
- Wedge: "turn matchdays into posts."
- Still weak: match intelligence until reviewed notes exist.

## Flashscore

- What they do better: fast scores across many competitions.
- What we do better: World Cup-specific fan identity and shareables.
- Why users add us: country-pride assets and local-time schedule cards.
- Wedge: all 48 teams get a card-ready road.
- Still weak: real-time updates.

## FIFA Official Site

- What they do better: official authority, fixtures, squads, tournament branding.
- What we do better: lightweight fan-made cards, captions, no official-brand dependency.
- Why users add us: fans can make shareable assets without needing official media.
- Wedge: rights-safe fan studio.
- Still weak: official data authority.

## Instagram/X Fan Pages

- What they do better: audience, personality, memes, existing trust.
- What we do better: fast reusable templates and country coverage.
- Why users add us: to generate posts faster and cover smaller teams.
- Wedge: fan-page toolkit potential.
- Still weak: personality and taste must be visually verified.

## Canva Templates

- What they do better: design flexibility and polished template marketplace.
- What we do better: football-specific data, team routing, captions, schedule context.
- Why users add us: less blank-canvas work, more match-specific output.
- Wedge: one-click World Cup card presets.
- Still weak: pure design quality may lag Canva.

## Telegram Reminder Bots

- What they do better: push reminders and automation.
- What we do better: visual schedule hub and share-card content.
- Why users add us: to pick teams and create social assets before reminders exist.
- Wedge: reminder sponsorship later, not first.
- Still weak: reminders are only planned.

## UI Wedge Check

The wedge is visible in the UI:

- Homepage: pick country, create fan card, 48 teams, no signup, local time, reminders planned.
- `/cards`: six large one-click templates first.
- `/teams`: country-first copy and every team has a hub.
- `/stats`: fan debate and share-card framing, not raw spreadsheet framing.
